Overview

You’re locking down a child’s iPad the way Apple locks down corporate devices. The winning combo is Supervision (via Apple Configurator for Mac) + a non‑removable configuration profile to disable account changes, the in‑Settings erase option, and random host pairing—while enforcing USB Restricted Mode. Then you layer Activation Lock and Screen Time for daily guardrails.

Prerequisites

  • A Mac with the latest Apple Configurator for Mac.
  • Lightning/USB‑C cable for the iPad.
  • Your Apple ID (parent/guardian) and your child’s Apple Account in Family Sharing.
  • Time to erase and set up the device fresh (this is a clean start).

Level 1 — Supervise with Configurator, then apply Screen Time + Activation Lock

This level blocks the tricks your kid used: signing out, wiping from Settings, or pairing to random computers.

  • Supervision unlocks the hard restrictions.
  • Restrictions Profile removes the in‑Settings erase path, account changes, and host pairing.
  • Activation Lock blocks activation after any restore without your Apple ID.
  • Screen Time provides day‑to‑day limits and “Accounts = Don’t Allow.”

A — Supervise with Apple Configurator

  1. Connect the iPad to your Mac and launch Apple Configurator.
  2. Select the device → click Prepare → choose Manual Configuration → check Supervise devices.
  3. (Optional) Save these settings as a Blueprint for future reuse.
  4. Finish the assistant to erase and supervise the device (clean slate).
Why Supervision?

It unlocks enterprise‑grade restrictions: block erase UI, lock account settings, and restrict computer pairing.

One‑time step

Once supervised, you can keep re‑applying profiles—and Activation Lock will protect against rogue restores.

C — Activation Lock & Screen Time

  1. On first boot, sign in with your Apple ID and enable Find My to arm Activation Lock.
  2. Then sign your child into your Family and their Apple Account on the device.
  3. On your iPhone/Mac, open Settings → Family → Screen Time → [Child] and set a Screen Time passcode known only to you.
  4. In Content & Privacy: set Accounts = Don’t Allow; consider blocking app install/deletion if you want a fixed app set.
Why this matters

If anyone tries a DFU/restore on a computer, Activation Lock blocks setup without your Apple ID. The profile already removed the in‑Settings erase path.

Level 2 — Optional: Automated Device Enrollment (ABM/ASM) + an MDM

Want “it always re‑locks itself after any erase”? Add the iPad to Apple Business/School Manager and assign it to a lightweight MDM (e.g., Jamf Now). After any wipe, during Setup Assistant the device auto‑enrolls, re‑pulls your profile, and supervision is mandatory.

  • Devices added to ABM via Configurator have a 30‑day provisional window where a user can leave management; after that, they can’t.
  • Buying the device directly into your ABM from Apple/resellers skips that 30‑day out entirely.

Recovery Playbook

  • In‑Settings erase attempt: The menu path is gone; nothing to do.
  • DFU/restore on a computer: Activation Lock appears. Enter your Apple ID to unlock.
  • Using ADE/MDM: After any erase, the device auto‑re‑enrolls and re‑applies your restrictions.

Keep your Mac admin creds and Configurator access private; that’s the only realistic way to remove Supervision.

Hand‑off Day Checklist

  • Settings shows “This iPad is supervised.”
  • Lockdown profile installed and marked non‑removable.
  • Screen Time passcode set; Accounts = Don’t Allow.
  • Find My enabled under your Apple ID → Activation Lock is armed.
  • USB Restricted Mode enforced (no accessories while locked).
