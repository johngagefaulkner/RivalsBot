COMPETITIVE SYSTEMS DECONSTRUCTED

An in-depth look at the Ranking & Matchmaking mechanics of Marvel Rivals

Ranking System

Your rank is adjusted after each match based on a weighted formula combining match outcome and personal skill.

Base Points

The foundation of your rank, determined by the match result and the skill difference between teams.

  • Win as Underdog: Large point gain.
  • Lose to Stronger Team: Small point deduction.
  • Win as Favorite: Small point gain.
  • Lose to Weaker Team: Large point deduction.

Performance Points

Recognizes your individual contribution, tailored to your hero's role and compared to others at your rank.

  • Role-Specific Metrics: Stats are normalized per-10-minutes.
  • Performance Coefficient: Your normalized stats are compared against the average for your hero at your rank.
  • Weighted by Rank: More impactful in lower ranks to help skilled players climb faster.

Matchmaking Algorithm

The system's goal is to create fair matches as quickly as possible by balancing three core factors.

Search Parameters

When you queue, the system initially searches based on:

  • Server Location
  • Competitive Score
  • Team Composition

Expansion & Balancing

As queue time increases, the search range for skill level widens. Once 12 players are found, teams are arranged to have the most balanced average score possible.

Team Rules

Strict rules apply to team sizes to maintain fairness:

  • 6-player teams only face other 6-player teams.
  • 5-player teams are disabled.
  • 4-player teams are restricted at high ranks.

Addressing Concerns

NO Engagement Optimized Matchmaking

The developers have explicitly stated the system does not manipulate matchmaking to enforce a 50% win rate or create artificial win/loss streaks.

Why Do Matches Feel Unbalanced?

  • Teamwork Factor: A coordinated team's synergy cannot be fully measured by a score average.
  • Role Flexibility: The absence of a role queue means players might be on unfamiliar heroes, impacting performance.

What About Win/Loss Streaks?

  • Statistical Probability: Streaks are a natural and statistically inevitable part of any competitive game.
  • In-Game Factors: Minor score imbalances or facing the same opponents can contribute to streaks.
